Instructions

You will hear test samples of computer generated speech created using different methods. The purpose of this test is to evaluate the quality of each test sample, so that we (the researchers) can compare the methods and know which ones sound better to a general audience.

 

Each test sample should be given a score according to the following scale, known as the MOS (mean opinion score) scale for naturalness:

Score Quality of the Speech Naturalness
5 Excellent Completely natural
4 Good Mostly natural
3 Fair Equally natural and unnatural
2 Poor Mostly unnatural
1 Bad Completely unnatural

 

Each test sample should also be given a pronunciation accuracy score, using the scale below.

Score Accuracy of the Speech Definition
5 Excellent No mispronunciations
4 Good Few minor mispronunciations
3 Fair Many minor mispronunciations
2 Poor Few major mispronunciations
1 Bad Many major mispronunciations
 

 

We have included a reference sample here which should rate highly on both scales (it may not be a perfect 5 on both).  You must transcribe this reference sample (this means write down what was spoken) in the given text box.  Do NOT transcribe the test sample!  We prefer that the transcription is translated to English, but it may be left in French.  Do not worry about spelling or punctuation. This step is only for validation and is not the main purpose of the study.

For each HIT, you will see the sentence for the audio test sample when you click the link below.  This should be useful for evaluating pronuncation accuracy.  You may find that some audio samples are missing words or even large parts of a sentence; these count as major mispronunciations.

There are a total of 40 test samples/HITs, and we prefer you do as many as you can. 

NOTE: The test sample and reference sample are different!  Here is the link to the reference sample.  The test sample can be found in the link in the HIT (it is not in the instructions).

Approval/Rejection Policy

To ensure that you know French well enough that you can evaluate the speech, we require that you also transcribe the reference sample.  If you decide to transcribe the test sample (which is linked below in the HIT), your HIT will be rejected, because YOU MUST TRANSCRIBE THE REFERENCE SAMPLE WHICH CAN BE FOUND INSIDE THE INSTRUCTIONS!

We have statistical methods to detect if you are randomly selecting scores and we will reject HITs that come from these individuals.

To obtain accurate results, we strongly recommend that you wear headphones and work in a quiet environment, otherwise you might not be able to discriminate between files with clearly different features.
